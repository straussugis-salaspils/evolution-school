# Pilot 18 — notes

## Variant A

- Grammar: explicit 2×2 decision matrix.
- Best insertion: after the article names the four reasons a practitioner may consider reinitiation.
- Strength: lets the reader compare the four situations at once and see that each has a different answer.
- Mobile: the quadrants become a single ordered sequence without losing the matrix numbering.

## Variant B

- Grammar: a central diagnostic question with four curved branches and broad labels around it.
- Best insertion: before the article’s practical checklist or school-specific explanation.
- Strength: changes the reader’s starting question from “where should I reinitiate?” to “what am I actually trying to do?”
- Each branch keeps its next step as a full-width second line rather than a narrow side column.
- The radial 2×2 composition is based on the illustration container width, not the browser viewport, so it remains stable inside a 760 px article insert.
- Mobile: the central question comes first and the four branches unfold as a connected vertical tree without box-like cards.

## Editorial safeguards

- Reinitiation is not presented as a universal requirement or cure.
- Returning to practice, reviewing materials and preparing for the next degree each receive their own non-reinitiation action.
- School-specific rules are clearly marked as school-specific.
- Evolution House policy is stated narrowly: transition from another lineage normally requires reinitiation, with a possible trusted-line exception.
- No medical, therapeutic or guaranteed-result claim is introduced.

## Recommendation

Variant A is best when the article needs a compact reference readers can scan later. Variant B is stronger as a behavioural intervention: the central question visibly produces four different endings, not one shared reinitiation endpoint.
